About Me

I am a researcher at Huawei's Central Software Institute working on LLM inference efficiency and LLM-based applications. I received my PhD in 2024 from Computer Science and Engineering, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. advised by Prof. Wei Wang. I also work closely with Dr. Ruichuan Chen and Prof. Bo Li. Prior to that, I got my Bachelor in 2019 from Department of Computer Science and Technology, Zhejiang University.

Current Topics: Natural Language Generation

  • LLM Inference Efficiency: Parallel decoding for AR LLMs; accelerate diffusion-based LLMs.
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation and AI Agents:
    • Domain-specific RAG-based question answering
    • Hey: A general-purpose, command-line AI agent with flexible tool calling and self correction abilities.

Topics in PhD: Privacy Preserving Machine Learning

  • Private Inference for LLMs:
    • Ensure system prompt privacy with minimized impact on conversational quality (PromptKeeper)
    • Replicate membership inference and data reconstruction attacks against LLMs for further investigation (released code)
  • Private and Efficient Federated Training:
    • Secure participant selection against adversarial servers (Lotto)
    • Enable dropout-resilient differential privacy without loss in time performance (Dordis)
    • Train asynchronously for end-to-end speedup (Pisces)

Dissertation: "Towards Private and Efficient Cross-Device Federated Learning"
